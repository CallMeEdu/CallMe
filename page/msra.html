MICROSOFT RESEARCH ASIA

A Data Visualization Authoring Tool in PowerBI

From Oct 2016 to April 2017, I spent a really exciting 6 months at Mircosoft Research Asia working on the PowerBI Custom Visuals. In the Software Analytics group, I worked with my mentor, Ray Huang, and the Principle Architecture, Haidong Zhang, to bring innovations to PowerBI through data-driven storytelling. There are two projects I have mainly worked on: PowerStory and Infographic Designer. Due to the NDA, I cannot disclose the details of these two projects. But the following is some takeaways that I can share, as well as my design reflections during the valuable 6 months.

PowerStory: Enable Non-Experts to Craft Narrative Visualizations

Design Process

The first challenge I was assigned was to design a visualization authoring tool in PowerBI for non-experts. Most of my time at MSRA was focused on prototyping and refining the details of the product. Thanks to my mentors for giving me enough freedom to think, execute, elaborate, and the opportunity to take an independent role in a product. Unfortunately, I cannot share any design work in this process. However, I can share what I have accomplished during the 6 months:

  • Conducted 24 Literature Review to understand the mechanism of Data-Driven Storytelling.
  • Conducted competitive analysis such as DataClips, Gapminder and Tableau to inform design.
  • Collaborated with researchers in the initial product planning, designed core features, information architecture, and user flow.
  • Finished rapid prototyping in Axure with 2 iterations based on team' feedback.
  • Designed UI with micro interactions.
  • Collaborated with my mentor in defining all the actions and designed 40+ animations to build the gallery for the product.
  • Designed style guide and collaborated with the developer to implement the demo.

Infographic Designer:
Beautify your Reports with easy-to-create infographics

Infographic Designer, released as a Power BI custom visual, enables easy creation of infographic visualizations for business users. It provides an easy-to-use UI for users to customize chart marks with rich visual elements, including icons, images, and texts.

Design Process

My second project was to refine the Infographic Designer, a current product in PowerBI Custom Visual. In this project, I got a chance to get a close contact with users by reading their feedback, complaints, as well as their suggestions for the product. Here is what I have done:

  • Summarized the user feedback to identify common issues and inform refinements.
  • Conducted a Heuristic Evaluation on its main features.
  • Simplified the workflow and user actions by re-organizing the main features and information architecture.
  • Designed examples on the product website to showcase what can be built through Infographic Designer and PowerBI.
