Prefill your loan application data with trusted data sources

THE PROBLEM

The loan application is cumbersome

When you apply for a home loan, no matter what lender you choose, you will have to go down the process of providing your personal information, listing your employeres, or even digging out a debt you have closed 5 years ago. This feels even worse because you have just provided the exact same information 2 months ago when you applied for an auto loan.

In Roostify, the loan application is broken down into 6 sections. This process can be tedious and cumbersome, and the more time it takes, the more likely a borrower will drop off the loan application.

+ Why is it such a big deal?

Lead generation is a critical part of Roostify's business: majority of our clients use Roostify as a lead generation tool. They would like to collect as many quality loans as possible. So the ability for the borrower to quickly submit a loan application is critical to the success of the business.

Additionally, when borrowers apply for a loan, chances are they will apply with multiple lenders in a bid to get money. An easier application process will gain us the advantage over our competitors.

THE OBJECTIVE

Speed up loan application with data prefill

The data required might have been previosly provided to the bank or via 3rd party data sources. Requesting it again is repetitive, and can lead to discrepancies with the user information across systems. We can further help borrowers complete loans applications by collecting data from external data sources automatically. With an easier application experience, we can lower the drop off rate and lenders are more likely to generate more leads.

Design Process

Understand the key design components

I started brainstorming and sketching out different design concepts to understand the key design components in this workflow. Through this exercise, I was able to idendify these following key components:


1. A onboarding process that introduces the prefill to the users


2. A buffer zone when the prefill is happening in progress


3. A mechanism that allows users to identify & review the prefilled fielda among others

Focusing on Reviewing Prefilled Information

One of the goals was to present the prefilled information at the first place so users can validate the data accuracy. I assumed that the transparency should be at the core of the design: when prefill happens, users would want to confirm whether the information prefilled is correct or not. My assumption, as well as stakeholders' feedback around compliance , was pushing the design into a certain direction that we should escalate the prefilled informatiom.

Design 1: Review in-App

The first design was a turbotax-ish summary page that lives within the application. User can review the prefilled information in its own section card. The "Go to section" CTA will take them to the individual sections where they fill out and edit information.

Design 2: Review pre-App

Another option was: before the user starts filling out the loan application, the user will see a separate page with all prefilled information that allows review or editing at a time.

Reframe the Problem

I tested the designs with 8 users who have recently purchased a home. While I was expectating a preference over one way or another, the feedback I received did go into a different direction.


A common theme from the user testings was: Users are not too concerned with whether the information is correct or not, since they know it comes from verified data sources. Their mindset was to complete the application as quickly as possible. They would like to know what has been completed, and what still remains. This calls for a big design shift from reviewing prefilled information, to helping them better track the loan progress.

Other major feedback also include: Users view the summary page as a checklist of how close they are to completion and what remains, and they would like to review all the information before submission or at the end of each section

"Is the prefilled information correct?"

"What do I still need to fill out?"

Better understand the loan progress

With the new design goal of better understanding the loan progress, the new design challenge is how to communicate the applicaiton status. The nature of our loan applications brings out these key considerations:


1. Not all fields are required to submit, but we would like to collect as much information as possible to generate high quality applications.


2. As users fill out the questions, more questions will be progressively revealed for further details.


Central to these charateristics, were these key design ideas:


1.The progress should be able to dynamically change along with the user input.


2.A balance between accuracy and obfuscation of the progress as users should feel encouraged but not pressed to complete the application.
